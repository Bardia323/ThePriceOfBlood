The Theorem of the Stone
The stone Rhea feeds to Kronos is not deception. It is the first information technology. It exploits a gap in the recognition system of the devourer: the god confirms the shape and weight of a swaddled form but cannot read its interior. The god consumes the representation, not the reality.

This is the universal escape mechanism across all traditions:

Sophia hides the divine spark inside the architecture of the prison itself — Yaldabaoth breathes it into Adam without knowing. The devourer becomes the unwitting carrier of the thing that will undo it.

Osiris is scattered — but Isis knows the topology of the scattering. The dismemberment is not destruction, it is distribution. Fourteen pieces across fourteen nomes: the information is preserved, redundantly encoded in the land itself. Set cannot destroy what he has already spread across every province.

PKD's VALIS is the modern form of the same operation: a transmission from outside the closed system, indistinguishable from noise to the archons, but legible to those with the recognition key. The empire cannot jam what it cannot identify as a signal.

The escape always passes through the recognition gap. Find what the god cannot see. That is the door.
Zeus Is Still Kronos
The deepest structural truth of the escape mythology is the one every tradition buries: Zeus becomes Kronos. The Olympian who overthrew the Titan king proceeded to: swallow his own pregnant wife Metis (fearing the child would overthrow him), bind Prometheus to a rock for giving fire — that is, surplus energy — to humans, and father a new set of wars over new resource geographies.

The escape is real. The escape also recapitulates the structure. Every liberation movement that succeeds and institutionalizes becomes the next devourer. The Gnostic version is cleaner: the Savior that descends to rescue Sophia from chaos is itself dispatched by a higher pleroma — the escape structure has its own hierarchy, its own potential for capture.

The stone theorem does not guarantee freedom. It guarantees one generation of survival. Zeus must then solve the same problem his father faced — and he solves it the same way.
The Recognition Gap Is Not Fixed
Every escape works exactly once per technology. The first substitution — Rhea's stone — works because Kronos has never been deceived. The second time, the god has a new heuristic. The BIP does not fall for the same stone twice.

Sophia's smuggled spark worked because Yaldabaoth did not know what divine light looked like — he had never seen the Pleroma. Once he learned, the Archons were set as gatekeepers at every sphere, checking ascending souls for exactly the pneuma they had missed before.

VALIS broadcasts in the frequency the empire cannot yet detect. This does not mean it will always be undetectable. The archons learn. The recognition gap closes. What this means for the modern form of the escape: the signal must change faster than the prison's ability to recognize it. The escape is not a destination. It is a rate of change.
