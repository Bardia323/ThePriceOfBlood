Energy method. The math is simple. Population × energy per person per year × years. We ran the numbers across 6,000 years of civilization. The machine has always needed fuel. It has always found some. The substrate changed; the hunger equation did not. Grain, wood, coal, oil. Different names, same formula. The accountant remains unbothered.
Blood volume. 5 liters per person. That's 1.32 gallons — roughly the size of a large gas station Slurpee. Your car's gas tank represents approximately 0.003 people. A barrel of oil: 158.99 liters of petroleum, or about 31.8 people's worth of blood. This is not a metaphor. This is a unit conversion.
Blood price. The modern system extracts $2.2M in energy value per liter of blood spilled. The medical market charges $880 for the same liter. Externality gap: 2,548×. The machine is not cruel. It is simply the most profitable business in the history of civilization, and it uses a currency it never has to list on the balance sheet.
